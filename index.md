I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences at Harvard University, working with [Susan Murphy](https://people.seas.harvard.edu/~samurphy/). I build AI/ML systems for healthcare and wearable sensing, with a focus on personalized mobile health interventions, online learning, reinforcement learning, Bayesian and survival modeling, time-series modeling, and reliable production deployment.

I received my Ph.D. in Data Science from the University of California San Diego, advised by [Tauhidur Rahman](https://www.tauhidurrahman.com/). My doctoral work developed wearable sensing, digital biomarker, and deep learning methods for understanding substance use and addiction, and my current work extends that line into uncertainty-aware decision making and deployed intervention systems.

<span style="background-color: #FFFF00;">I am currently on the job market for AI/ML engineering, applied scientist, and research scientist roles.</span> I am especially interested in teams building reliable AI systems for healthcare, wearables, mobile sensing, and personalized decision support. If your work intersects with these areas, please get in touch.

Previously, I worked as a research intern at [Optum AI Labs](https://www.optumlabs.com/work/artificial-intelligence.html) and [Samsung Digital Health Lab](https://www.sra.samsung.com/digital-health/), and as a software engineer at Samsung R&D Institute. I joined the University of Massachusetts Amherst as a Master's student in 2017, moved to the MS/Ph.D. track in 2018, and later transferred to the University of California San Diego, where I completed my doctorate in 2024. I did my undergrad at the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati (IITG), where I completed my thesis under the supervision of [Samit Bhattacharya](http://www.iitg.ac.in/samit/) and [Benny George](https://www.iitg.ac.in/ben/).
